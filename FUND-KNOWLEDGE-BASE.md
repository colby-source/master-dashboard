## 4. Investment Thesis — Why Affordable Housing, Why Now

### The Government Tailwind (2026 Data)

**Section 8 Funding — Historic Increase:**
- FY2026 Tenant-Based Rental Assistance: **$38.4B** (a $2.4B increase from FY2025)
- Project-Based Rental Assistance: **$18.5B** ($2.05B increase)
- 60,000 new Housing Choice Vouchers distributed
- Total HUD budget: **$77.3B** — a $7.2B increase over FY2025
- Congress rejected the President's proposed $33B in HUD cuts and INCREASED funding instead

**LIHTC Expansion — Permanent Law (One Big Beautiful Bill Act):**
- Permanent 12% increase in 9% LIHTC state allocations starting Jan 1, 2026
- Bond financing threshold permanently reduced from 50% to 25% (massive unlock)
- Novogradac estimates: **1.22 million additional affordable rental homes over 2026–2035**
- Expected to generate $71.4B in tax revenue and support 1.83M jobs
- Public Welfare Investment cap raised from 15% to 20% of bank capital (more bank LIHTC investment)

**Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac — Record Capacity:**
- 2026 multifamily loan purchase caps: **$88B each** ($176B combined) — up 20.5% from $146B in 2025
- At least 50% must be mission-driven affordable housing
- FHFA doubled LIHTC investment authority to **$2B each annually** ($4B total)

### The Demand Crisis

- **7.2 million affordable rental home shortage** (NLIHC Gap Report 2026)
- Only 35 affordable homes available for every 100 extremely low-income households
- 74% of 11 million extremely low-income renters are severely cost-burdened (50%+ of income on rent)
- Overall U.S. housing supply gap: **4.03 million homes** and growing
- Section 8 waitlists: 4–8+ years in major metros; applications close within hours when opened
- 5+ million households currently served — demand far exceeds supply

### Class B/C Outperformance (2026 Market Data)

| Metric | Class A | Class B | Class C / Value-Add |
|--------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Rent Growth | Flat | +1.7% | +3.4% / +5.38% |
| Cap Rate Trend | Least compression | Moderate | 5–8 bps compression |
| Occupancy | Under pressure (concessions) | Mid-90s% | Mid-90s% (outperforming A) |

Class A is oversupplied from new construction. Class B/C affordable housing is undersupplied and outperforming on every metric.

### New Legislation Supporting the Thesis

- **Executive Order (March 2026):** "Removing Regulatory Barriers to Affordable Home Construction"
- **Senate bipartisan housing bill (March 12, 2026):** Restricts institutional investors from buying single-family homes; eases affordable development regulations
- **21st Century ROAD to Housing Act:** Bipartisan House + Senate housing reform package
- New supply pipeline shrinking — starts declining, setting up stronger fundamentals late 2026–2027

- **Dollar-for-dollar offsets** against federal tax liability (NOT deductions)
